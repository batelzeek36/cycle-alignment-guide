Cycle Alignment Guide

Self-Care · Creativity · Leadership
Day 1 = first day of full bleeding
● Menstrual Phase Day 1–5 (or while bleeding)
Reset · Release · Restore
Body cues: Bleeding, low energy/fatigue, cramping or heaviness, more emotional or inward, desire for quiet and solitude
Self-Care
Lifestyle
  • Slow your schedule
  • Prioritize rest & gentle mornings
  • Light movement: walking, stretching, yin yoga
Nutrition
  • Iron-rich foods (beef, lamb, bone broth)
  • Warm meals and drinks
  • Hydration + electrolytes
Support
  • Heat (bath or heating pad)
  • Early bedtime
  • Journaling or reflection
Creative & Work
Best For
  • Visioning and big-picture ideas
  • Brand direction & creative concepts
  • Reviewing what's working / not
  • Strategy and financial reflection
Filming
  • Avoid if possible
  • Script ideas, voice notes, content planning
Social & Leadership
Social Energy
  • Low — keep interactions minimal or 1:1
Retreat Leadership
  • Not ideal for hosting
  • Excellent for retreat design & improvement planning
◈ Follicular Phase Day 6–Ovulation (typically Day 6–13)
Build · Initiate · Expand
Body cues: Energy rising, clear thinking and focus, motivation to organize or start new things, mood improving, cervical fluid minimal or creamy
Self-Care
Lifestyle
  • Plan, organize, and start projects
  • Schedule meetings or collaborations
  • Try new routines or ideas
Movement
  • Strength training
  • Moderate cardio
Nutrition
  • Balanced meals with protein
  • Fresh, lighter foods
  • Stable blood sugar for sustained energy
Creative & Work
Best For
  • Starting creative projects
  • Writing content
  • Brainstorming campaigns
  • Building systems or offers
  • Learning new tools or skills
Filming
  • Educational content
  • Tutorials
  • Informational or value-based videos
Social & Leadership
Social Energy
  • Moderate — great for meetings & collaboration
Retreat Leadership
  • Planning retreats
  • Vendor coordination
  • Structuring schedules & experiences
✦ Ovulatory Phase Around mid-cycle (12–16 days before next period)
Express · Connect · Lead
Body cues: Increased confidence and social energy, higher libido, clear stretchy cervical fluid, skin may appear brighter, desire to connect and be seen
Self-Care
Lifestyle
  • Be visible and active
  • Schedule important conversations
  • Engage socially
Movement
  • Higher-intensity workouts
  • Performance-based activity
Nutrition
  • Balanced meals with protein & healthy fats
  • Stay well hydrated
Creative & Work
Best For
  • Personal brand expression
  • High-visibility creative work
  • Launches or promotions
Filming
  • Primary filming days
  • Photoshoots
  • Promotional content
  • Interviews, podcasts, speaking
Social & Leadership
Social Energy
  • Highest of the cycle — networking & events
Retreat Leadership
  • Best phase for hosting retreats
  • Group facilitation
  • Guest connection
  • Ceremonies, experiences, sales conversations
☙ Luteal Phase Ovulation to day before next period (last 12–14 days)
Refine · Protect · Prepare
Body cues: Energy gradually declining, increased appetite or cravings, bloating or breast tenderness, greater emotional sensitivity, lower stress tolerance
Self-Care
Lifestyle
  • Finish & refine vs. starting new
  • Simplify your schedule
  • Create calm, organized environments
  • Strengthen boundaries
Movement
  • Moderate workouts early luteal
  • Walking, Pilates, yoga later
Nutrition & Support
  • Higher protein and healthy fats
  • Magnesium-rich foods (dark chocolate, nuts)
  • Regular meals for stable mood
  • Earlier bedtime; reduce caffeine/alcohol
Creative & Work
Best For
  • Editing content
  • Refining brand visuals
  • Completing projects
  • Administrative work & follow-ups
  • Quality control
Filming
  • Light filming early luteal if needed
  • Avoid heavy camera days late luteal
  • Ideal for editing & post-production
Social & Leadership
Social Energy
  • Moderate early luteal
  • Lower later — prefer smaller groups
Retreat Leadership
  • Good for smaller, intimate retreats (early)
  • Avoid high-demand hosting late luteal
  • Best for behind-the-scenes operations

How to Mark Your Calendar

  1. Day 1 = first day of full bleeding
  2. Menstrual: Day 1 through last bleed day
  3. Luteal: Count back 12–14 days from next period
  4. Ovulation: The day before luteal begins
  5. Follicular: All days between bleeding and ovulation
Example (28-day cycle): Day 1–5 Menstrual • Day 6–13 Follicular • Day 14 Ovulation • Day 15–28 Luteal

Strategic Reality Check

  • Filming late luteal often feels "off"
  • Hosting retreats late luteal risks burnout
  • Menstrual phase = clearest strategic insight
  • Ovulation = strongest presence (but may overcommit)
The power isn't tracking your cycle. The power is scheduling your life around your nervous system.
